## üî• Headlines

<WebGPULogo /> In the context of Google IO ([web
talks](https://io.google/2023/program/?q=keynote,technical-session,web-topic,web)), Google presented
its support of WebGPU in Chrome with the article [**Unlocking modern GPU access in the
browser**](https://developer.chrome.com/en/blog/webgpu-io2023/), the video [**Introducing
WebGPU**](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m6T-Mq1BPXg) with [Corentin
Wallez](https://twitter.com/DaKangz) and [Brandon Jones](https://twitter.com/Tojiro), and the
[**Your First WebGPU App**](https://codelabs.developers.google.com/your-first-webgpu-app) codelab.

Brandon Jones was on the Voices of VR podcast [**Primer on
WebGPU**](https://voicesofvr.com/1213-primer-on-webgpu-bringing-high-performance-3d-graphics-and-parallel-compute-to-the-web/)
with [Kent Bye](https://twitter.com/kentbye), and [mcc](https://mastodon.social/@mcc) wrote the excellent [**I want to talk about WebGPU**](https://cohost.org/mcc/post/1406157-i-want-to-talk-about-webgpu), which is great to learn about the history of WebGPU and graphics APIs.

WebGPU already received [performance improvements in Chrome 114](https://developer.chrome.com/blog/new-in-webgpu-114/), and [**WebTransport**](https://developer.chrome.com/en/articles/webtransport/) is now [enabled by default in Firefox 114](https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=1709355).
