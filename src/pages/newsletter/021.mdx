## üî• Headlines

üìÖ [**GDC**](https://gdconf.com/) is coming up next week and we have an official [**Web Game Dev GDC meetup**](https://partiful.com/e/LyzI2xkHAcgkPwal9Squ) organized by [Trezy](https://twitter.com/TrezyCodes) (who has been reviving our Discord events ‚ù§Ô∏è). RSVP and get in touch in the new **#gdc** Discord channel!

If you are attending GDC, make sure to come say hi to [**Poki at their booth**](https://twitter.com/Poki/status/1757752535312404606) (S657) and meet the [13 teams](https://twitter.com/Poki/status/1759943410994041111) (!) of game devs they are bringing to the event. There is also a [**Courage XL**](https://courage.events/event/couragexl2024/) pre-GDC event where [CrazyGames](https://twitter.com/CrazyGamesCom) co-hosts a web game booth. They have free drinks and VIP tickets! Message @Rafael_CrazyGames on our Discord.

üá™üá∫ The [**Digital Markets Act**](https://open-web-advocacy.org/blog/the-digital-markets-act-is-in-force-what-happens-now/) is now in force, which means that _gatekeepers_ such as Apple, Google, Meta, and Microsoft must now comply with the new rules. The act aims to create a fair and competitive digital market by preventing gatekeepers from abusing their power. As a reponse to the DMA, Apple tried last week to kill PWAs entirely in the EU and reversed their decision after being met with backlash led by the [Open Web Advocacy](https://open-web-advocacy.org/blog/apple-backs-off-killing-web-apps/). The DMA should be beneficial to web games by allowing browser engines other than WebKit on iOS, encouraging browser diversity, and bringing web apps closer to native apps in terms of features (at least in the EU).

The [**results of the Gamedev.js Survey 2023**](https://gamedevjs.com/survey/2023/) are now available!
