## Core Concepts

- **Vectors** — the fundamental building block. A vector represents a direction and magnitude (e.g., velocity, force, position offset). Operations like addition, subtraction, dot product, and cross product are used constantly.
- **Matrices** — used to represent transformations (translation, rotation, scale) and combine them efficiently. A 4x4 matrix can encode an object's entire transform.
- **Quaternions** — the preferred way to represent rotations in 3D. Unlike Euler angles, they don't suffer from gimbal lock and interpolate smoothly.
- **Lerp** (linear interpolation) — smoothly blends between two values. `lerp(a, b, t)` returns a value between `a` and `b` based on `t` (0 to 1). Used everywhere: movement smoothing, color transitions, camera follow.
- **Smoothstep and damp** — non-linear interpolation for smoother, more natural transitions than raw lerp.
- **Trigonometry** — `sin`, `cos`, and `atan2` are essential for circular motion, rotations, angle calculations, and wave-based animations.
- **Projections** — converting between 3D world space and 2D screen space (and back).
