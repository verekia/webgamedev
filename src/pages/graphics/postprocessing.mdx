# 🌈 Postprocessing

Post-processing effects are applied to the fully rendered frame as a series of 2D image operations. Instead of modifying the 3D scene directly, you render the scene to a texture, then pass that texture through one or more **effect passes** that modify the final image.

## Common Effects

- **Bloom** — makes bright areas glow by blurring and adding them back to the image
- **Ambient Occlusion** (SSAO) — darkens creases and corners where ambient light would be occluded, adding depth
- **Depth of Field** — blurs objects outside a focal distance, simulating camera focus
- **Tone Mapping** — maps HDR (high dynamic range) values to the displayable range, affecting overall look and contrast
- **Outline** — highlights selected objects or interactive elements with an edge effect
- **Motion Blur** — blurs in the direction of movement for a sense of speed
- **Vignette** — darkens the edges of the screen for a cinematic look

## Effect Pipeline

Effects are chained together in a **composer** — each pass reads the output of the previous one and writes its result. Order matters: applying tone mapping before bloom gives different results than the reverse.
